Fertility Problem Due to the Use of Inorganic Fertilizers

Long-term use of inorganic fertilizers can lead to the degradation of soil structure and fertility. These fertilizers often focus on a limited range of nutrients, primarily nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), neglecting other essential micronutrients like zinc, magnesium, and calcium. As a result, soils can become deficient in these nutrients over time.

Our Solution

InsectUp Pro Fertiliser ,the product of larvae feeding on organic waste, is a rich source of organic matter and balanced nutrients, including micronutrients often absent in inorganic fertilizers. It improves soil structure by increasing organic matter content, which enhances soil aeration, water retention, and microbial activity. This leads to long-term soil fertility improvement.

Food Waste

In Africa, an estimated 37% of food produced is lost or wasted post-harvest or during processing, which is approximately 100 million tons annually. In East Africa, food waste is a growing problem, especially in urban areas where infrastructure for waste management is limited, leading to open dumping and increased greenhouse gas emissions.

Our Solution

BSFL-based waste treatment operations have been shown to reduce waste volumes in urban centers by 30-40%, mitigating methane emissions from decomposing organic waste. The use of BSFL can prevent tons of waste from reaching landfills, contributing to cleaner cities and improved waste management systems.
